Interests

I am interested in understanding (i) how organs are sculpted and positioned within embryos and (ii) the molecular origin of forces acting between and within tissues to affect each other's behaviour during development. I study these in drosophila embryos.

They say, seeing is believing. So, I love to work with 4D images that show us the how tissues and cells change shape or move in space and time and how subcellular entities like cytoskeleton, adhesion proteins and organelles reorganise to bring about the cellular and tissue behaviour. I always get excited about extracting data from images and integrating digital image and a biological understanding of whats going on in that image. I plan to base my career on images, whether I continue in academia or decide to move to the industry. Although I have used software tools that support automated 2D and 3D segmentation, I am keen to understand the underlying algorithms and implementation details. I believe this deeper understanding will help me design methods better suited to my specific use cases.

Projects

A few projects that shaped the way I think about forces, tissues, cells, and images.

TIFR Mumbai PhD research

Cell-matrix interactions during tissue unfolding

My doctoral work studies how cells connect to the surrounding extracellular matrix, and how those connections help tissues move during germband retraction in Drosophila embryos. The project brings together developmental biology, mechanics, and image-based analysis of living tissues.

NUS Internship + thesis

NUS internship and thesis in 3D collagen models

At the Mechanobiology Institute, National University of Singapore, I worked on co-culture systems and later developed my master's thesis around how mechanical interactions between fibroblasts and epithelial cells can change epithelial cell shape and nuclear behaviour in a wound-like 3D environment.

Master's thesis

IISER Pune Research foundations

Nuclear and cellular mechanobiology

During my BS-MS years, I worked on cellular models for nuclear mechanobiology, studied stem-cell differentiation, and explored how physical constraints and cell state can affect cellular behaviour. These projects built the experimental base for my later work on tissue mechanics.

iGEM 2017 Silver medal

TB or Not TB

As part of the IISER Pune iGEM team, I helped represent the institute at the Giant Jamboree in Boston. The team worked on a synthetic biology approach for faster and more accessible tuberculosis diagnostics, and won a silver medal.
